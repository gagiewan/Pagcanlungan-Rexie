What is Valentine's Day?

Valentine's Day, celebrated on February 14th, is a day dedicated to expressing affection, admiration, and appreciation for loved ones. Originally associated with Saint Valentine, it has evolved into a cultural and commercial celebration of love. Common customs include sending greeting cards, giving flowers (especially roses), and sharing chocolates, making it a special occasion to celebrate romantic love, friendship, and family bonds. The day encourages people to express how much they care for those who hold a special place in their hearts.
